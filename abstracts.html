Farsighted cheap talk on networks (Abstract)

We developed a farsighted network formation mechanism driven by communication. Therefor existing cheap talk models were generalized for multiagent and multisignal setup, as enriched by endogenous audience partition and signal retrasmitting. As a result, we found out that audience partition is defined by a number and magnitude of inversions in agent orders regarding to their biases and informedness. Interaction of senders is propelled by their mutual alignment on a set of biases, since it leads to differences in order of receivers. Timing of messages and signal production are valid actions to deal with inversions. We defined one-short and stationary policies, as developed asymptotic properties of network formation. New model provides a reasonable structure for communication networks, as well as a practical way of network analysis based on balance of intragroup influences.

Bayesian persuasion in tax competition (Abstract)

International tax competition is a game with incomplete information. Besides tax rates governments do not know actions neither of other governments nor of taxpayers, that makes trustful communication the issue of key importance. Hence, we employ Bayesian persuasion mechanism to simulate information exchange between governments. As a result we demonstrate how communication can be used to facilitate tax avoidance, why level of tax rates cannot be used to fence countries that benefits from tax planning, how some income source countries can benefit from tax avoidance, and provide reasoning for tax network configurations that exist in practice. Our model reveals inefficiency of common tax rate policy with limited countries or information exchange incentives. We consider provision of technical support as a valid instrument to alleviate the problem.
