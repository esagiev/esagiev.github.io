Welcome

I am a PhD student at the Department of Economics of the University of Essex. Before switching to academics, I was a practicing tax accountant, tax consultant, and tax policy advisor to the Government of Kazakhstan, non-government organizations, and the World Bank.

In 2003, I graduated from the Saint Petersburg State University of Economics with Diploma in Economics. In 2016, I received Master of Research in Economics with distinction in the University of Essex.

Research:

Game theory with particular interest in algorithmic game theory and communication games on networks. Currently, I work on application of reinforcement learning to multi-agent systems in economics.

Chapters of my PhD thesis:

Farsighted noisy communication in dynamic networks (Abstract)
Bayesian persuasion in tax competition (Abstract)
Bayesian persuasion mediating cheap talk (Abstract, SSRN)

Programming languages I largely use are Python, Matlab, and R. I also have experience in using Git and Docker.
